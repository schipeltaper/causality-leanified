\begin{proof}
We verify the chained equality componentwise. It suffices to show
$\lp G_{\doit(W_1)} \rp_{\doit(W_2)} = G_{\doit(W_1 \cup W_2)}$; the second equality
$\lp G_{\doit(W_2)} \rp_{\doit(W_1)} = G_{\doit(W_1 \cup W_2)}$ then follows by the
same argument with $W_1, W_2$ swapped. By def\_3\_10 each side of the targeted
equality is a CDMG, presented as a 4-tuple $(J_\ast, V_\ast, E_\ast, L_\ast)$;
we check the four components in turn.

\medskip\noindent\emph{Input nodes.}
$(J \cup W_1) \cup W_2 = J \cup (W_1 \cup W_2)$, by associativity of $\cup$. \checkmark

\medskip\noindent\emph{Output nodes.}
$(V \sm W_1) \sm W_2 = V \sm (W_1 \cup W_2)$, by the standard set identity
$(A \sm B) \sm C = A \sm (B \cup C)$. \checkmark

\medskip\noindent\emph{Directed edges.}
\begin{align*}
  E_{\lp G_{\doit(W_1)} \rp_{\doit(W_2)}}
  &= \lp E \sm \lC v \tuh w \mid w \in W_1 \rC \rp
     \sm \lC v \tuh w \mid w \in W_2 \rC \\
  &= E \sm \lC v \tuh w \mid w \in W_1 \cup W_2 \rC
   = E_{\doit(W_1 \cup W_2)}. \; \checkmark
\end{align*}

\medskip\noindent\emph{Bidirected edges.}
\begin{align*}
  L_{\lp G_{\doit(W_1)} \rp_{\doit(W_2)}}
  &= \lp L \sm \lC v \huh w \mid w \in W_1 \rC \rp
     \sm \lC v \huh w \mid w \in W_2 \rC \\
  &= L \sm \lC v \huh w \mid w \in W_1 \cup W_2 \rC
   = L_{\doit(W_1 \cup W_2)}. \; \checkmark
\end{align*}

All four components of $\lp G_{\doit(W_1)} \rp_{\doit(W_2)}$ agree with those of
$G_{\doit(W_1 \cup W_2)}$, giving the first equality. Swapping the roles of $W_1$
and $W_2$ in the argument above (using $W_1 \cup W_2 = W_2 \cup W_1$ in the
recombined component identities) yields the second equality
$\lp G_{\doit(W_2)} \rp_{\doit(W_1)} = G_{\doit(W_1 \cup W_2)}$, completing the chain.
\end{proof}
