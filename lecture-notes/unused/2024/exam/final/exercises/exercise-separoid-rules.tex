\subsection{Separoid Rules}
\totalscore{12}

In the following let $\Xcal$, $\Ycal$, $\Zcal$, $\Wcal$, $\Ucal$ and $\Tcal$ be standard measurable spaces and $K(W|T) : \Tcal \dshto \Wcal$ be a Markov kernel.
Furthermore, we consider corresponding conditional random variables:
\begin{align}
    X: \Wcal \times \Tcal &\to \Xcal, &
    Y: \Wcal \times \Tcal &\to \Ycal, &
    Z: \Wcal \times \Tcal &\to \Zcal, 
\end{align}
and so on, and let $T: \Wcal \times \Tcal \to \Tcal$ be the canonical projection onto the second argument, and let $\Asterisk$ be the constant map.
We write $X \Indep Y | Z$ for the conditional independence w.r.t.\ $K(W|T)$.

Recall that we write: $X \precsim Y$ iff there exists a measurable map $f:\Ycal \to \Xcal$ such that $X = f \circ Y$. We also write: $X \approx Y$ iff $X \precsim Y$ and $Y \precsim X$.

Prove the following implications:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\alph*)]
    \item $T \precsim Z \implies X \Indep T |Z$,
      \score{4}
    \item $U \precsim (Y,Z)$ and $X \Indep Y|Z \; \implies \; X \Indep U,Y|Z$,
      \score{2}
    \item $U \approx Z$ and  $X \Indep Y |Z \;\implies\; X \Indep Y|U$.
      \score{6}
\end{enumerate}
\emph{Hint: All those rules can be proven only with help of the standard (asymmetric) separoid rules and the following (trivial) observation:
\begin{align}
    U \precsim Z \implies U \precsim (Y,Z).
\end{align}
You are also allowed to use rule b) to prove rule c) above.
}

\bigskip
For your convenience we list here all (asymmetric) separoid rules for conditional independence:
\begin{enumerate}
	\item Extended Left Redundancy: 
    %\item[] 
        $U \precsim X \implies U \Indep  Y \given X$.
    \item $T$-Restricted Right Redundancy:
    %\item[] 
        $X\Indep \Asterisk|Z,T$ always holds.
    \item $T$-Inverted Right Decomposition:
    %\item[] 
        $X \Indep  Y \given Z \implies X \Indep  T, Y \given Z$.
	\item Left Decomposition:  
	%\item[]
        $X,U \Indep  Y \given Z \implies U \Indep  Y \given Z$.
  \item Right Decomposition:  	
    %\item[] 
        $X \Indep  Y,U \given Z \implies X \Indep  U \given Z$.
    \item Left Weak Union:
    %\item[] 
        $X , U \Indep  Y  \given Z \implies X \Indep  Y \given U , Z$.
  \item Right Weak Union: 
  %\item[] 
      $X \Indep  Y , U \given Z \implies X \Indep  Y \given U , Z$.
\item Left Contraction:
    %\item[] 
 $ (X \Indep  Y \given U , Z) \land (U \Indep  Y \given Z) \implies X , U \Indep  Y \given Z$.
\item Right Contraction:
    %\item[] 
    $ (X \Indep  Y \given U , Z) \land (X \Indep  U \given Z)  \implies X \Indep  Y , U \given Z$.
\item Right Cross Contraction:
    %\item[] 
    $ (X \Indep  Y \given U , Z) \land (U \Indep  X \given Z) \implies X  \Indep  Y , U \given Z$.
\item Flipped Left Cross Contraction: 
    %\item[] 
    $ (X \Indep  Y \given U , Z) \land (Y \Indep  U \given Z) \implies Y \Indep  X , U \given Z$.
\end{enumerate}

%%%%%%%%%%%%%
\answer{
\points{Each needed separoid rule gives 1pt.}

a) We have the implications:
\begin{align}
    & \stackrel{T\text{-R.R.Red.}}{\implies} X \Indep \Asterisk |Z,T.\\
    T \precsim Z & \stackrel{\text{E.L.Red.}}{\implies} T \Indep X | Z.\\
    \lp X \Indep \Asterisk |Z,T \rp \;\land\; \lp T \Indep X | Z \rp & \stackrel{\text{R.C.Contr.}}{\implies} X \Indep T,\Asterisk | Z \\
    &\stackrel{\text{R.Dec.}}{\implies} X \Indep T |Z.
\end{align}


b) We have the implications:
\begin{align}
%    & \stackrel{T\text{-I.R.Red.}}{\implies} X \Indep T,Y|Z. \\
%    U \precsim Z &\stackrel{\text{O}}{\implies} U \precsim (Y,Z) \\
  U \precsim (Y,Z)  & \stackrel{\text{E.L.Red.}}{\implies} U \Indep X | Y,Z. \\
\lp U \Indep X | Y,Z \rp \; \land \;\lp X \Indep Y|Z \rp    & \stackrel{\text{F.L.C.Contr.}}{\implies} X \Indep U,Y|Z.
\end{align}

c) We have the implications:
\begin{align}
    U \precsim Z & \stackrel{\text{O}}{\implies} U \precsim (Y,Z). \\
    \lp U \precsim (Y,Z) \rp \;\land\;   \lp X \Indep Y |Z \rp &\stackrel{\text{point }(2)}{\implies} X \Indep U,Y|Z \\
    &\stackrel{\text{R.W.Union}}{\implies} X \Indep Y|U,Z. \\
    Z \precsim U & \stackrel{\text{E.L.Red.}}{\implies} Z \Indep Y |U.\\
    \lp X \Indep Y|U,Z \rp \;\land\; \lp Z \Indep Y|U \rp & \stackrel{\text{L.Contr.}}{\implies} X,Z \Indep Y|U \\
    &\stackrel{\text{L.Dec.}}{\implies} X \Indep Y |U.
\end{align}


}
