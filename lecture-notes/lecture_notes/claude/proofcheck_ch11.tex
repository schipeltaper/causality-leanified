\section{Proof 3: Prp.\ \texttt{prp:sigma-inducing\_path\_properties} (lines 218--256)}

\textbf{Analysis.} (i)$\Rightarrow$(ii): trivial. (ii)$\Rightarrow$(iii): minimal

\section{Proof 7: Lem.\ \texttt{lem:inducing\_path\_out\_of\_input} (lines 325--330)}

$\checkmark$

\section{Proof 16: Lem.\ \texttt{lem:possep} (lines 1267--1287)}
\textbf{Statement.} $\mathrm{SEP}_G(i,j)\subseteq\mathrm{PosSEP}_{H_0}(i,j)$.

\textbf{Analysis.}
The proof considers a collider $b$ on the witnessing walk and claims the
corresponding unshielded triple in $H_0$ ``will have been oriented as a
definite collider'' (line~1281).

\textbf{GAP (significant):} By Prp.~13, if $b\in\mathrm{Anc}(\{a,c\}\cup S)$
(Case~ii), then $b$ is in \emph{every} separating set and the triple is NOT
oriented as a collider.  The proof does not rule out this case.  If it occurs
and $a,c$ are non-adjacent, the $\mathrm{PosSEP}$ condition fails.

\textbf{Verdict: GAP.}

\textbf{Key issues:}
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Proofs 3--6 (systematic minor gap):}
    Walk-to-path transitions with endpoint orientation preservation are
    asserted without formal justification.  The claims are all correct
    but the step from ``$\sigma$-inducing walk into $j$'' to
    ``$\sigma$-inducing \emph{path} into $j$'' needs an argument.
  \item \textbf{Proof~16 (Lem.\ \texttt{lem:possep}), line~1281:}
    The proof claims a collider triple is ``oriented as a definite collider''
    but does not rule out Case~(ii) of Prp.~13, where the collider is in
    $\mathrm{Anc}(\{a,c\}\cup S)$ and hence in every separating set (so NOT
    oriented as a collider).  This propagates to Proofs~17--18.
  \item \textbf{Proof~18 (Thm.\ \texttt{thm:fci\_sound}), R9/R10:}
    Application of Lem.~20 (\texttt{lem:upd\_path}) lacks verification of the
    arrowhead hypothesis at $v_1$.  Fixable via proof by contradiction.
  \item \textbf{Proof~14, line~993:} ``$\in$'' should be ``$\subseteq$''.
\end{enumerate}
